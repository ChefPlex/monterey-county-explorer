  <div class="intro">
    <p>South of the Santa Cruz Mountains, the coastal fog pushes inland through one of the most remarkable agricultural corridors in the country — a valley that feeds the nation its salad and pours some of California's most exciting cool-climate Pinot Noir. This is Monterey County: the Salinas Valley's lettuces and strawberries at eye level; the Santa Lucia Highlands' Burgundy-challenged vineyards etched into the mountain terraces above. The slow food traditions here aren't performative — they're baked into the soil, into Swiss dairy families who've been here since 1944, into abalone cages swaying beneath a commercial wharf. Come with an appetite and a corkscrew.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="slow-food-box">
    <h3>🐌 Slow Food in This Region</h3>
    <p>Monterey County is connected to the broader <strong>Slow Food California</strong> network and the <strong>Slow Food South Bay</strong> chapter, which has organized seed-saving events, One Seed One Community programs, and farm-to-fork gatherings at venues like Asilomar on Monterey Bay. The region also draws on the deep slow food ethos embedded in <em>Edible Monterey Bay</em> — the area's essential quarterly publication — and organizations like <strong>Everyone's Harvest</strong> and the <strong>Monterey Bay Certified Farmers Markets</strong>, which have served the Central Coast since 1976. Restaurants and producers who lean into the Slow Food philosophy include:</p>
    <ul>
      <li>Support for small-scale, family-run farms and dairies</li>
      <li>Sourcing from the county's 15+ certified farmers markets</li>
      <li>Championing Monterey County's biodiversity — from artichokes to abalone</li>
      <li>Preserving heritage breeds and heirloom seeds through events like EcoFarm at Asilomar</li>
    </ul>
  </div>

  <div class="ava-box">
    <h3>About the Santa Lucia Highlands AVA</h3>
    <p>Established in 1992, the SLH occupies the eastern flank of the Santa Lucia Mountains in central Monterey County — roughly 6,000 acres of vine planted on alluvial terraces between 1,200 and 2,200 feet elevation. Morning fog pours in from Monterey Bay; afternoon winds roar down the Salinas Valley. This thermal drama is responsible for California's most Burgundy-competitive Pinot Noir and Chardonnay. Most tasting rooms are located in Carmel-by-the-Sea and Carmel Valley, with a handful of estate experiences on River Road/County Road G17.</p>
  </div>

      <p class="card-desc">One of only a handful of artisan cheesemakers in the Monterey Bay area, operating as a California Artisan Cheese Guild member. Worth seeking out at local farmers markets for small-batch, regionally distinctive cheeses.</p>
      <span class="card-tag tag-craft">Artisan Cheese · Guild Member</span>

      <p class="card-location">1 Esquiline Road, Carmel Valley Village</p>
      <p class="card-desc">French-born Sophie Hauville opened her small-batch goat and cow's milk creamery on Bastille Day 2024, apprenticed under legendary Big Sur cheesemaker Charlie Cascio. She makes chèvre, gruyère, herbed cheeses, and local honey boards at what used to be Rosie's Cracker Barrel — a very Slow Food origin story.</p>
