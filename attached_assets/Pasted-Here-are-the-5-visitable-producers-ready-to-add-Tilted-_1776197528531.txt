**Tilted Shed Ciderworks**
Category: Producer / Craft Beverage
City: Windsor
Address: 7761 Bell Road (Artisan Alley)
Hours: Fridays 2–7pm, Saturdays 12–5pm
Website: tiltedshed.com
Note: Sonoma County's benchmark natural cidermaker, working with heirloom and organic apples since 2011. The tasting room pours flights, by the glass, or by the can in a no-frills warehouse space that feels nothing like a winery — which is exactly the point. Slow Food Snail of Approval. Kids and dogs welcome.

**The Epicurean Connection**
Category: Producer / Culinary Experience
City: Sonoma
Address: 19670 8th St East
Hours: Warehouse/shop open Friday and Saturday 9am–12pm. Cheesemaking classes Thursday, Friday, and Saturday at 12–2pm and 3:30–5:30pm.
Website: theepicureanconnection.com
Note: Sheana Davis and Ben Sessions have been making award-winning cheese and teaching cheesemaking classes in Sonoma for over 30 years. The shop carries their cheeses, local jams, olive oil, meats, and pantry staples. The hands-on classes — ricotta, butter, fresh chèvre — are one of the best culinary experiences in the county. Slow Food Snail of Approval.

**Lala's Jam Bar and Urban Farmstand**
Category: Producer / Specialty Food Shop
City: Petaluma
Address: 720 E. Washington Street
Hours: Friday to Monday, 10am–5pm
Website: lalasjams.com
Note: The only dedicated jam shop in Sonoma County — a little yellow cottage on Washington Street where Leslie Goodrich makes small-batch, low-sugar jams from locally gleaned fruit. Sunday jam-making classes available. Also stocks curated local Sonoma County food products. Slow Food Snail of Approval.

---

**Ambix Spirits**
Category: Producer / Craft Beverage
City: Sebastopol
Address: 1944 High School Road
Hours: By appointment, Fridays, Saturdays, and Sundays 12–4pm
Website: ambixspirits.com
Note: David Klein's Calvados-style apple brandy distillery on a 4-acre Sebastopol orchard planted with 200+ heritage apple varieties. Tastings are $15 and include a short orchard and distillery tour. One of the most distinctive and least-known stops in West County. Slow Food Snail of Approval.

**Maison Porcella**
Category: Producer / Restaurant
City: Windsor
Address: 8499 Old Redwood Highway, Suite 114
Hours: Retail store Tuesday–Saturday 10am–6pm. Lunch Tuesday–Saturday 12–2pm; dinner Tuesday–Saturday 5:30–7pm, by reservation.
Website: maisonporcella.com
Note: Franco-American chef Marc-Henri Jean-Baptiste's authentic French charcuterie shop and bistro in Windsor. House-made pâté, boudin blanc, cassoulet, grab-and-go croque monsieur pastries, and a curated selection of French and local wines. Also at Healdsburg and Sonoma farmers markets. Slow Food Snail of Approval. Add to both Producers and Restaurants.
